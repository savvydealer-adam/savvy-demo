Service FAQ

Ford recommends oil changes every 7,500 to 10,000 miles for most models when using full-synthetic oil, or as indicated by your Intelligent Oil-Life Monitor. However, if you drive in severe conditions -- frequent short trips, dusty roads, towing, or extreme temperatures -- you may need more frequent changes. Our service team can help you determine the right interval based on your specific Ford model and driving habits.
While you're not required to service exclusively at a Ford dealership, there are significant advantages. Any warranty claims are processed faster when your service history is on file with an authorized Ford dealer.
Our complimentary multi-point inspection covers all major vehicle systems: engine oil and filter condition, brake pad thickness and rotor condition, tire tread depth and pressure, all fluid levels (coolant, transmission, power steering, brake, washer), battery health and terminal condition, belt and hose integrity, exhaust system, suspension components, and all interior and exterior lights. You'll receive a detailed report card showing green (good), yellow (may need attention soon), or red (needs immediate attention) for each item.
You can check for open recalls on your Ford by visiting owner.ford.com and entering your VIN (Vehicle Identification Number), or simply call our service department at (813) 752-4171 and we'll look it up for you. Recall repairs are always performed at no charge to you. When you bring your vehicle in for any service, we automatically check for open recalls and can complete them during the same visit to save you an extra trip.
